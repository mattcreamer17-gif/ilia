The shortlist problem

CI&T, Globant, Softtek, ília.

The question is what separates ília from CI&T, Globant, and Softtek in a shortlist, and the honest answer from the outside is: not very much yet. The partner logos are the same. The service lines read the same. The headcount is in the same band.

What ília does not have on the public record is a published methodology that says, in ília's words, how ília does this work. That is a solvable gap.

The frame we work from

Most AI engagements load the LLM with 100 percent of the problem. In practice, the split is closer to this.

60%

Traditional code & database

CRUD, joins, deterministic APIs. The workhorse layer. Postgres and .NET do this faster, cheaper, with better guarantees.

30%

Rule-based logic

Business rules, compliance gates, eligibility trees. Readable by a risk committee. Auditable by a regulator.

10%

Genuine AI problem

Where natural language, ambiguity, or unstructured context actually earn the LLM spend.

Apex AscendOS is the back end. ília is building the front end. The orchestration layer between them is where the methodology lives.

The methodology

Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM)

Peer-reviewed (submitted to ACM TiiS), MIT-licensed, already deployed inside a 52-member practitioner community. The core claim: agent context organized as a layered filesystem (L0 identity through L4 working artifacts) produces measurable interpretability and reproducibility gains.

For a services firm whose next 18 months rest on differentiating inside a commoditizing category, ICM is a wedge that lives in your deliverables, not in someone else's partner directory.
